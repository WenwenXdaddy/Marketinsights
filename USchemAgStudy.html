Part V

Conviction Hierarchy & Deep Dives

#1 HIGHEST CONVICTION

CF Industries (CF) — $131

World's largest ammonia producer · 40% of North American nitrogen market
+76% YTD
S&P 500's top performer

Bull case: Mechanical margin expansion — revenue at global urea price ($683/mt), cost at HH ($2.90). FY2025 adjusted EBITDA of $2.89B (+27% YoY). 97% capacity utilization. Blue Point ammonia plant for structural growth. Prediction markets: only 19.5% chance of conflict resolution by Mar 31.

Bear case: Valuation stretch vs $105–110 consensus. Mizuho downgrade to Underperform. DOJ antitrust probe. Insider selling: 25 sales / 0 buys in past year (ex-CEO sold $6.27M on Mar 9). Peak-earnings multiple compression risk. Rapid de-escalation collapses premium.

#2 LyondellBasell (LYB)

$75.20 — +74% YTD

Thesis: US ethane cracker advantage + European pivot. CFO confirmed April order books "strongest in several months." Citigroup double-upgraded. KeyBanc sees 5–10% global PE supply tightening.

Risk: European operations are a drag; LYB declared force majeure on EU polyolefin production. Recession volume risk if downstream demand buckles.

#3 Dow Inc. (DOW)

$39.47 — +47% YTD

Thesis: Broad petchem beneficiary. CEO Fitterling: "Ethane fundamentals haven't changed, but oil fundamentals have changed dramatically." Citi upgraded to Buy at $40.

Risk: Diversification dilutes leverage. Construction/automotive weakness. Q1 earnings April 23 will be first confirmation.

#4 Corteva (CTVA)

$80.99 — +16% YTD (ATH)

Indirect beneficiary via ag spending cycle. Proprietary portfolio provides pricing power. Corn-to-soy shift increases herbicide demand. But UBS & JPMorgan downgraded to Neutral; less direct Hormuz leverage.

#5 LSB Industries (LXU)

Small-Cap Nitrogen Leverage

Higher beta exposure to nitrogen price spike. Less institutional coverage, less liquid. Small scale but pure-play thesis. Suitable for aggressive tactical positioning only.

Scenario Analysis

Bear — Quick Resolution

Even in this case, Citi argues months of residual supply-driven pricing from logistics bottlenecks, insurance normalization, and unit restarts. Oil Dec futures still $79.70 (+10% above pre-war).

Base — Q3 Partial Reopening

Hormuz: Partially restricted through Q3
Brent: $95–110 range
Urea: Sustains $600–750/mt
CF: $120–140 range with Q1 earnings catalyst
Probability: ~50%

Consistent with DIA assessment, oil backwardation, and China maintaining export bans through August. Spring/summer planting season keeps nitrogen demand elevated.

Bull — Prolonged / Escalation

Hormuz: 2027+ closure or widening
Brent: $120+ (Kharg seizure → higher)
Urea: $800+ (approaching 2022 peak)
CF: $150+ with structural re-rating
Probability: ~30%

Iran published list of 32% of regional olefin supply as retaliation targets. Houthi warned against Hormuz reopening efforts. Recession risk rises sharply at these oil levels.

China Export Policy — The Swing Variable

Current status: Beijing banned exports of NK fertilizer blends, reiterated urea restrictions. 50–80% of China's fertilizer exports (~40M mt) now restricted. Only ammonium sulfate can still be exported. Industry sources at Shanghai conference: bans not expected to lift before August.

Strategic calculus: BMI: "China restricts supplies rather than coming to the rescue during global tightness." CSIS: "China is so reluctant to do anything that would increase the price of grains, especially animal feed, domestically." India has formally requested urea export quotas from China. If China maintains restrictions through H2, the bull case for nitrogen pricing becomes significantly more probable.
